Research Interests

Exploring the intersection of genomics and conservation biology

Conservation Genomics

Applying genomic tools and techniques to understand genetic diversity, population structure, and adaptive potential in threatened and endangered species. This research helps inform evidence-based conservation strategies and management decisions.

Population Genetics

Investigating genetic variation within and among populations to understand evolutionary processes, gene flow patterns, and the impacts of habitat fragmentation on genetic diversity. This work is crucial for maintaining healthy, viable populations.

Biodiversity Preservation

Using genomic data to assess biodiversity at multiple scales, from genes to ecosystems. This research aims to identify priority areas for conservation and understand the genomic basis of species' responses to environmental change.

Wildlife Genomics

Developing and applying genomic methods to study wildlife populations, including population monitoring, kinship analysis, and identifying adaptive genetic variation. These tools help track species' responses to environmental pressures and inform recovery programs.

Research Approach

My work combines field ecology, laboratory genomics, and computational biology to address fundamental questions in conservation science. I collaborate with conservation practitioners, wildlife managers, and local communities to ensure research outcomes have real-world impact.
